## Weekly Insights
- **W14 (Agent Moved into the OS)**: Four companies simultaneously moved from "advising" to "operating" — Claude controlling Mac, Gemini automating phone apps, Codex plugging into enterprise workflows. Intercom's Fin Apex 1.0 proved that post-trained vertical models beat frontier general models in specific domains (73.1% vs 71.1% resolution rate), signaling that base models are becoming replaceable components for enterprise use cases.
- **W15 (The Math Doesn't Work)**: Agent sessions consume 50-100x more tokens than conversations but users pay the same subscription — the business model foundation collapsed. Intuit's data (300M users, 85% reuse, 30% reduction in manual work) showed the most commercially successful agents are the most "restrained" ones, optimizing token efficiency over raw autonomy.
- **W16 (No One Is Verifying)**: Q1 2026 AI funding exceeded all of 2025, 29% of Fortune 500 became paying AI startup customers, and Anthropic launched Managed Agents with four enterprise customers — but Berkeley simultaneously proved all major agent benchmarks can be gamed. Enterprises are making procurement decisions based on scores that may not predict production performance.
- **W17 (Context War)**: Salesforce, Shopify, SAP, and ServiceNow chose to open their platforms as agent infrastructure rather than fight AI companies directly — betting that their accumulated customer context becomes more valuable when agents can programmatically access it. The strategic question for every enterprise SaaS platform: lock down data, self-build models, or open up as AI infrastructure.
