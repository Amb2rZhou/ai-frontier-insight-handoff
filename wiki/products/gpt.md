## Key Developments

### GPT-5.x Evolution
GPT-5.3-Codex went GA (Feb 25). GPT-5.4 series launched with Thinking variant, mini ($0.20/M input tokens), and nano variants for agent workloads (Mar 18). GPT-5.4 Image Encoder updated (Mar 14). GPT-5.4-Cyber for cybersecurity launched (Apr 15).

### Specialized Models
GPT-Rosalind debuted for life sciences research with partners including Amgen, Moderna, Allen Institute, Thermo Fisher Scientific (Apr 17).

### ChatGPT
Reached 900M weekly users (Feb 28). ChatGPT Images 2.0 with major multimodal capabilities released (Apr 22).

### Safety
CoT-Control evaluation suite released, with GPT-5.4 Thinking scoring 0.3% controllability. However, independent research "Reasoning Theater" questioned CoT faithfulness.

## Weekly Insights
- **W11 (Safety Evaluation Credibility Crisis)**: GPT-5.4 launched with native computer use and 1M context, and OpenAI wrote CoT controllability scores (0.3%) into its System Card as a safety metric — but the "Reasoning Theater" paper published the same week showed CoT can be performative, not faithful, raising questions about this safety backing.
- **W13 (Water Sellers' Water Getting Cheaper)**: GPT-5.4 mini ($0.20/M input tokens) and nano launched at dramatically lower prices — not from generosity but to preempt customer migration to open-source alternatives. OpenAI explicitly promoted a tiered architecture (GPT-5.4 for planning, mini/nano for sub-agents) to create ecosystem lock-in through multi-model dependencies.
- **W17 (Context War)**: GPT-Rosalind debuted for life sciences (partnered with Amgen, Moderna) and GPT-5.4-Cyber for cybersecurity — demonstrating the "one reasoning engine, many domains" expansion where vertical knowledge comes from partnerships, not from scratch training.
