## Weekly Insights
- **W10 (Controllability Replaces Capability)**: MatX raised $500M as an NVIDIA challenger, and Wayve raised $1.2B for embodied AI backed by NVIDIA — signaling NVIDIA's dual role as both dominant incumbent and ecosystem enabler.
- **W11 (Safety Evaluation Credibility Crisis)**: Jensen Huang announced pulling back from strategic investments in OpenAI and Anthropic, shifting to neutral infrastructure positioning — indicating NVIDIA sees Agent-era certainty as high enough to stop picking winners among model companies.
- **W12 (Model Race to Habitat War)**: Open-sourced Nemotron-3-Super (120B agent model) and began building an open-source agent platform — the move that most clearly demonstrated model capability becoming commoditized infrastructure, like Linux for servers.
- **W13 (Water Sellers' Water Getting Cheaper)**: Launched NemoClaw enterprise agent platform and Nemotron-Cascade 2 (30B MoE, only 3B activated, near-frontier performance) at GTC — constructing a "chips + runtime + model + orchestration" full stack where the model is deliberately designed as the replaceable component, and the orchestration/security layers become the real lock-in.
- **W15 (The Math Doesn't Work)**: NemoClaw's enterprise agent platform remained in alpha but represented the alternative to "Agent tax" — enabling companies to run high-capability agents locally on their own hardware, bypassing commercial API costs entirely.
